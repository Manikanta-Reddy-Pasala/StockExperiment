Strategy Overview
EMA 200 / EMA 400 Crossover (1H)

Trend-following strategy on the 1H timeframe. Detects trend by EMA 200 crossing EMA 400, then waits for retest confirmation before entering. Multi-entry pyramid: Entry 1 on EMA 200 retest break, Entry 2 on EMA 400 retest break. Single stop at 1H close past EMA 400.

Stage Chain:
Trend Identification

EMA 200 crosses EMA 400: above = BUY trend, below = SELL trend

State machine locks the crossover candle and waits for break-and-close above its high (BUY) or below its low (SELL).

Retest 1 → First Entry

Price retests EMA 200 from the opposite side, then breaks the retest candle's high (BUY) or low (SELL) and sustains.

Selection score: 100. This is the high-conviction entry.

Retest 2 → Second Entry (Pyramid)

After Entry 1, price pulls back to EMA 400. Break of the new retest candle triggers Entry 2.

Selection score: 90. Lets the system pyramid into a strong move.

Risk Management
  • Stop Loss: 1H close past EMA 400 (closes ALL open entries)
  • Target: 5000 absolute pts (indices) or 1:3 RR (equity)
  • Time Frame: 1H bars; entries hold hours-to-days
  • Backtest: 31.6% WR, 2.77:1 R:R, +9429 net P&L per unit (Fyers, 504 NSE Nifty 500, 720d)

Entry Rules
  1. Crossover (Trend ID): EMA 200 crosses above EMA 400 → BUY trend (mirror for SELL)
  2. First Alert: 1H close breaks crossover candle's high (BUY) or low (SELL)
  3. Second Alert (Retest 1): Price retests EMA 200 from opposite side, then breaks retest candle → Entry 1
  4. Third Alert (Retest 2): Price pulls to EMA 400, retest break → Entry 2 (pyramid)
  5. Universe: Nifty 500 only; min 405 1H bars (~80 trading days) for warmup
Exit Rules
  1. Target Hit: Bar high (BUY) or low (SELL) reaches the configured target.
    • Indices: 5000 absolute pts
    • Equities: 1:3 risk-reward (3 × distance from entry to EMA 400)
  2. Stop Loss: 1H close past EMA 400 (BUY: close < EMA 400; SELL: close > EMA 400). Closes all open entries together.
  3. Trend Flip: Opposite EMA crossover resets state machine and exits.
  4. Pyramiding: Multiple Entry 2 retests allowed per cycle (pyramid loop) — disabled if you want single-entry mode.
